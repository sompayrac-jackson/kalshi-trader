### Two distinct problem zones:

**Zone 1 (50–65%):** 86 bets where model says 50–65% but actual WR is 35–49%. These should mostly not be taken. The model is overconfident on marginal signals.

**Zone 2 (70–80%):** The most alarming finding. 34 bets where the model says 70–80% confidence but actual WR is 40–47%. This is not noise — it's a systematic failure. Most likely cause: the Markov model sees a strong score state and assigns high probability, but the market already knows the lead is there. Our "edge" comes from a few percentage points of mispricing, but the model is overestimating by 25–30 points in these regimes.

**Zone 3 (65–70%, 80–85%):** Well-calibrated. These are the entry targets.

**Action: set min_model_prob to 65%** (already available in Settings slider).

## Why Dry Run Is Performing Worse

Dry run shows negative P&L even in the 10–20% edge bucket (−$109.24 vs +$490.36 for live). This is a measurement artifact: live and dry share `_open_tickers`. When live buys a market, dry is blocked from entering it. Dry ends up evaluating a different (worse) subset of opportunities. The dry numbers are **not** a clean independent test of the strategy.

## Model Improvement Hypotheses (Next Session)

- **Early vs late game confidence requirement** — require higher min_model_prob in Set 1 / innings 1–3 vs later. The model has more variance early when the score hasn't differentiated.
- **Score state integration** — the Markov model currently treats "Top 3, 1-0 score" the same as "Top 3, 4-0 score." Run differential and game lead should shift probability more aggressively in mid-game.
- **Opponent quality signal** — Kalshi pricing implicitly encodes Vegas lines; if our model consistently overestimates underdogs in the 20%+ edge bucket (38.7% actual WR), adding a prior toward the market price in extreme cases would improve calibration.
- **Why 70–80% model prob underperforms** — this is the biggest open question. May indicate the model overfits to scoreline patterns that don't predict final outcomes at that confidence level.
